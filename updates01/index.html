Our Belief

Teaching is not a job.
It is the most heroic act
a civilization can ask of its people.

Heroes are not born. They are forged through challenge, through mentorship, through systems designed to demand and develop the best in them. Most school systems were built to manage educators, not to grow them. We built something different.

And when educators grow with intention, mastering the science of learning, the design of learning, and the engineering of learning, something changes in every classroom they walk into. Students stop being sorted. They start being seen. Their learning becomes personal. Their pathway becomes theirs.

"Educators who thrive don't just stay longer.
They see their students differently. That's the connection we build for."

"America is not facing an educator shortage.
It is facing an educator ecosystem failure."

David B. Palumbo  &  Adam Rubin  ·  Education Accelerated & 2Revolutions

The Problem We Solve

Most systems set educators up to fail before they begin.

The industrial model of education was never designed to grow people. It was designed to sort them. Educators enter one of the most demanding professions on earth and are handed a key, a roster, and a wish of good luck.

1.

No Pipeline

Schools wait for certified teachers instead of growing their own. The heroes are out there, in high schools, in communities, and the system ignores them. Every unfilled classroom is a learner without a guide.

2.

Sink or Swim Induction

New educators are handed a classroom and left to figure it out. The most formative years of a teaching career are also the most unsupported, and students in those classrooms absorb every cost of that isolation.

3.

No Path Forward

Veterans plateau. There is no structural investment in the educator who has been teaching for fifteen years and still has fifteen more to give. When great teachers stop growing, their students stop getting the best of them.

Thriving educators.
Thriving learners.
We know how to build both.

Teaching dyads, one mentor teacher paired with one apprentice, consistently produce higher rates of student growth and proficiency than even the most experienced solo classrooms. That's not a theory. That's what happens when you invest in the relationship structure of teaching, not just the individual.

We don't position ourselves as the hero of this story. You are. Your educators are. We're the guide who knows the terrain, holds the framework, and refuses to let the hero fail for lack of support.

Our Frameworks

Structured. Targeted. Proven.

Every EA engagement is structured around two proprietary frameworks. They are what separate a consulting visit from a lasting change in how a system operates.

FUSION
Innovative Planning Process
The alternative to traditional strategic planning. EA's Think Tank model brings every voice into the room, board members, teachers, parents, and students, and produces a prioritized action architecture in days, not years. Systems that have tried conventional planning and found it too slow, too top-down, or too forgettable use FUSION instead.
KeySTONE
Structural · Timeless · Organizational · Non-negotiable · Ecosystem
The model that separates structural priorities from noise. Not every initiative is a KeySTONE move. The ones that are correctly identified reshape how an entire system allocates its energy. KeySTONE keeps leadership focused on what will still matter in ten years.
Global Community of Practice
The Learning Never Stops
Residency is the beginning, not the end. EA connects educators across schools, systems, and countries, creating an ongoing professional community that supports every stage of the Arc. An induction teacher in Colorado and a mentor teacher in Panama participate in the same professional conversation. A building leader in Guatemala learns alongside a system leader in New Zealand. True professional mastery is built in community, sustained across a career.

Building the Destination District

Aurora Public Schools  ·  Aurora, Colorado

Aurora Public Schools serves students from more than 130 countries who speak over 160 languages, one of the most diverse urban school systems in Colorado. When Superintendent Michael Giles arrived with a mandate to make APS a "Destination District," Education Accelerated was engaged to build the educator development infrastructure that vision requires.

The Challenge

A destination district is not declared. It is built. APS needed more than a vision. It needed the structural foundation for system-level leadership alignment, shared commitment, shared language, and the working relationships that turn a bold aspiration into a coordinated reality.

EA's Role

Education Accelerated brought the FUSION Process and Think Tank methodology to APS, convening district leadership around a shared strategic direction, building the collaborative infrastructure that makes alignment stick, and designing the development architecture that carries educators from aspiration through excellence.

"This is important work that I personally prioritize. Education Accelerated brought the clarity and structure we needed, a roadmap that makes building a true destination district feel not just visionary, but doable."

Michael Giles, Jr.  ·  Superintendent, Aurora Public Schools

From Possibility to Purpose

Colegio Americano de Guatemala  ·  Future of Learning Think Tank

As CAG marked its 80th year, nearly fifty stakeholders, board members, school leaders, teachers, parents, and students, gathered not because something was broken, but because something important was at stake. The question was not "Are we good enough?" It was harder and more honest: Who do we need to become for the next generation of learners?

The Process

Education Accelerated's FUSION Process and IDEAL Framework structured three days of work: Day One challenged participants to reimagine learning for 2035. Day Two moved from system critique to human development, redesigning learning around identity, wellbeing, and agency. Day Three turned ideas into architecture, identifying which moves were structural enough to reshape the whole ecosystem.

Igniting Global Leadership

AAIE Think Tank  ·  21 Countries  ·  5 Continents

In January 2025, Education Accelerated partnered with the Association for the Advancement of International Education (AAIE) to host a Think Tank at their annual meeting in Washington, DC. Forty international school leaders from 21 countries and five continents convened, not to attend a conference, but to design one together.

The central question: How can educational leaders turn moments of opportunity and moments of friction into catalysts for sustainable innovation?

The Process: FUSION and ABCQ in Action

EA's FUSION Process structured the experience across three phases: pre-work grounded in real school challenges, a two-day collaborative Think Tank using the ABCQ model (Assets, Barriers, Connections, Questions), and a personalized post-event innovation playbook every leader carried home.

Key Insight: Connectors and Disruptors

Participants arrived believing they were either connectors or disruptors. By the end of day one, they recognized they needed to be both, knowing when to rally a community around shared vision and when to challenge the system to prevent complacency.

"We brought together a group of individuals. We left with a network of like-minded, passionate leaders committed to working together as both connectors and disruptors, leading education forward."

Participant  ·  AAIE Think Tank  ·  January 2025
